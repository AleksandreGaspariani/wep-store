Pistols

Pistols are versatile firearms designed for one-handed use. They come in various calibers and are favored for self-defense, concealed carry, and sport shooting. Popular types include semi-automatic and revolvers, with different features for accuracy, capacity, and ergonomics. Always prioritize safety and training when handling pistols.

Shotgun

Shotguns are powerful firearms known for their wide pellet spread, making them effective for close-range shooting and versatile applications. They come in various styles, including pump-action, semi-automatic, and break-action. Shotguns are used for hunting, home defense, and sport shooting, with different gauges for various purposes. The ammunition typically includes birdshot, buckshot, and slugs, each suited to different tasks. Always follow safety guidelines when handling shotguns.

Hunting Rifle

Hunting rifles are firearms specifically designed for hunting game animals. They are typically bolt-action, lever-action, or semi-automatic, with a variety of calibers to suit different game and distances. Hunters choose rifles based on factors such as caliber, accuracy, and recoil control. Proper training and knowledge of local hunting laws are essential for safe and responsible hunting with rifles.

Rifle

A rifle is a long-barreled firearm designed for accuracy and distance. It's typically used with two hands, providing stability for precise shooting. Rifles come in various types, such as bolt-action, semi-automatic, and lever-action, and they use a wide range of calibers. They serve various purposes, including hunting, sport shooting, and military applications. Rifles are prized for their versatility, accuracy, and power, and safety practices are crucial when handling them.

Melee

Melee weapons are close-quarters combat tools designed for use without projectiles. These weapons include swords, knives, clubs, axes, and more. They have been used throughout history for self-defense, hunting, and warfare. Melee weapons vary in design and purpose, offering different advantages and disadvantages, such as reach, weight, and cutting ability. They require physical skill and close proximity to the target, making training and caution essential when using them.

Ammo

Ammunition is the essential component that firearms use to function. It typically consists of a cartridge, which includes a casing, primer, propellant (gunpowder), and a bullet, shot, or projectile. Ammunition comes in various calibers and gauges, each suited to specific firearms. The type of ammunition is matched to the firearm for compatibility and safety. Ammunition serves diverse purposes, such as self-defense, hunting, and sport shooting, and various types are designed for specific applications, including target practice, hunting game, or personal protection. Proper storage, handling, and knowledge of local regulations are essential when dealing with ammunition.

Attachments

Attachments commonly regarded as combat accessories include extended magazines, dual magazine clamps, lights, lasers, shell saddles and certain kinds of optics. In essence, any attachment that enables the shooter to fire more quickly and accurately, or to reload less often, could be a good fit for a combat weapon.
